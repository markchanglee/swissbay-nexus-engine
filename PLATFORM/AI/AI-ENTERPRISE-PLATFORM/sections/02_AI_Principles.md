# 02 — AI Principles

---

# Overview

The AI Principles define the architectural and ethical foundations governing every Artificial Intelligence capability within Swissbay.

These principles apply consistently across AI agents, copilots, prompt engineering, memory, retrieval, reasoning, automation and industry-specific intelligence.

Every AI capability must align with these principles before being introduced into the platform.

---

# Purpose

The AI Principles exist to:

- establish a common AI philosophy
- guide AI architecture
- support governance
- improve trust
- preserve security
- encourage responsible innovation
- enable long-term scalability

These principles are foundational to the Swissbay AI Enterprise Platform.

---

# Principle 1 — AI Assists

Artificial Intelligence exists to assist people.

AI should augment human capability rather than replace accountable decision-makers.

Recommendations remain subject to human review where organisational policy requires.

---

# Principle 2 — AI Explains

Every recommendation, prediction or generated output should be explainable.

Users should understand:

- why a recommendation was made
- what information influenced it
- the confidence level
- any limitations

Explainability strengthens enterprise trust.

---

# Principle 3 — AI Obeys Governance

AI operates within:

- business rules
- security policies
- regulatory requirements
- workflow approvals
- organisational standards

AI must never bypass governance mechanisms.

---

# Principle 4 — AI Uses Canonical Knowledge

AI consumes:

- canonical Business Objects
- Metadata Registry
- Knowledge Graph
- governed enterprise memory

AI should never rely on unmanaged or unauthorised enterprise information.

---

# Principle 5 — AI Learns Responsibly

Continuous improvement is encouraged through:

- evaluation
- monitored feedback
- governance review
- version management

Learning should always remain observable and controlled.

---

# Principle 6 — Human Accountability

Ultimate responsibility for enterprise decisions remains with authorised people.

AI may recommend actions but should not independently approve:

- contracts
- legal decisions
- employment decisions
- financial commitments
- regulatory submissions

Critical actions require human oversight.

---

# Principle 7 — Security by Design

AI inherits the Swissbay Security Architecture.

AI must respect:

- identity
- permissions
- data classifications
- encryption
- audit requirements

Security applies consistently across every AI capability.

---

# Principle 8 — Model Independence

Swissbay is independent of any single AI provider.

The platform should support:

- OpenAI
- Anthropic
- Google
- Microsoft
- DeepSeek
- local enterprise models

AI architecture remains stable while models evolve.

---

# Principle 9 — Observability

Every AI interaction should be measurable.

Examples include:

- latency
- cost
- confidence
- tool usage
- model selection
- user feedback

Observability enables continuous optimisation.

---

# Principle 10 — Continuous Evaluation

AI quality must be monitored continuously.

Evaluation includes:

- accuracy
- hallucination detection
- policy compliance
- factual consistency
- business relevance
- user satisfaction

Evaluation should occur throughout the AI lifecycle.

---

# Principle 11 — Enterprise Reuse

AI capabilities should be reusable across:

- Business Objects
- Platform Services
- Industry Packs
- Enterprise Applications

Intelligence should be shared rather than duplicated.

---

# Principle 12 — Future Readiness

Swissbay AI should remain adaptable to:

- new reasoning techniques
- emerging model architectures
- autonomous agents
- enterprise memory
- digital twins
- future AI capabilities

The architecture should evolve without redesigning the platform.

---

# Architectural Decision Framework

Every AI capability should answer:

1. Does it improve enterprise value?
2. Is it governed?
3. Is it explainable?
4. Does it use canonical information?
5. Can it be reused?
6. Is it secure?
7. Can it be evaluated?
8. Does it preserve human accountability?

If any answer is "no", the capability should be reconsidered.

# Summary

The AI Principles establish the enduring philosophy of the Swissbay AI Enterprise Platform.

By embedding governance, explainability, security, human accountability and enterprise reuse into every AI capability, Swissbay creates a trusted and sustainable foundation for enterprise intelligence that remains adaptable to future technologies while preserving architectural integrity.
