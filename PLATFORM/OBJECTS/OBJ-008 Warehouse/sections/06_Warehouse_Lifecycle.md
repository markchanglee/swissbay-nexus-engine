# 06 — Warehouse Lifecycle

---

# Overview

The Warehouse Lifecycle defines the governed progression of every Warehouse from planning through operation and eventual decommissioning.

Every Warehouse follows a standard lifecycle that ensures consistent governance, operational reliability, regulatory compliance and enterprise visibility.

A Warehouse exists in one—and only one—lifecycle stage at any given time.

---

# Purpose

The Warehouse Lifecycle exists to:

- standardise warehouse governance
- improve operational consistency
- strengthen compliance
- optimise warehouse performance
- support Artificial Intelligence
- improve lifecycle visibility
- maximise operational value

The lifecycle provides one operating model for enterprise warehouse management.

---

# Lifecycle Overview

Every Warehouse progresses through the following stages.

```text
Planning

↓

Design

↓

Construction / Configuration

↓

Commissioning

↓

Operational

↓

Optimisation

↓

Expansion / Reconfiguration

↓

Decommissioning

↓

Archived
```

Warehouses normally remain in the **Operational** stage for most of their lifecycle.

---

# Stage 1 — Planning

## Purpose

Identify the need for a Warehouse.

### Activities

- business justification
- capacity planning
- location selection
- operational requirements
- investment approval

### Exit Criteria

Warehouse project approved.

---

# Stage 2 — Design

## Purpose

Design the warehouse layout and operating model.

### Activities

- storage design
- warehouse zoning
- workflow planning
- safety planning
- technology planning

### Outputs

Approved warehouse design.

---

# Stage 3 — Construction / Configuration

## Purpose

Construct or configure the Warehouse.

### Activities

- facility construction
- shelving installation
- warehouse systems
- network configuration
- operational readiness

### Outputs

Warehouse ready for commissioning.

---

# Stage 4 — Commissioning

## Purpose

Prepare the Warehouse for operational use.

### Activities

- operational testing
- safety inspections
- process validation
- staff training
- acceptance

### Outputs

Warehouse approved for operation.

---

# Stage 5 — Operational

## Purpose

Support enterprise operations.

### Activities

- goods receipt
- storage
- picking
- packing
- goods issue
- warehouse transfers
- inventory management

This is normally the longest lifecycle stage.

---

# Stage 6 — Optimisation

## Purpose

Improve warehouse performance.

### Activities

- layout optimisation
- process improvement
- automation
- capacity optimisation
- AI recommendations

Optimisation is continuous throughout warehouse operations.

---

# Stage 7 — Expansion / Reconfiguration

## Purpose

Modify warehouse capability.

### Activities

- warehouse expansion
- storage changes
- technology upgrades
- operational redesign

Warehouse identity remains unchanged.

---

# Stage 8 — Decommissioning

## Purpose

Retire warehouse operations.

### Activities

- inventory relocation
- facility shutdown
- equipment removal
- operational closure

Warehouse history remains governed.

---

# Stage 9 — Archived

## Purpose

Retain historical warehouse information.

Archived warehouses:

- remain searchable
- support audits
- support reporting
- support AI learning
- cannot be modified without governance approval

---

# Lifecycle Events

Major lifecycle events include:

- Warehouse Planned
- Warehouse Approved
- Warehouse Commissioned
- Warehouse Activated
- Warehouse Expanded
- Warehouse Optimised
- Warehouse Closed
- Warehouse Archived

These events trigger workflows, automation and dashboards.

---

# AI Throughout the Lifecycle

Artificial Intelligence may:

- optimise layouts
- forecast capacity
- recommend automation
- predict warehouse demand
- identify bottlenecks
- recommend operational improvements

AI remains advisory throughout the lifecycle.

---

# Governance

Every lifecycle transition requires:

- validation
- approval where applicable
