# 02 — Purpose

---

# Overview

The Warehouse Business Object exists to provide a single, governed representation of every enterprise warehouse throughout its operational lifecycle.

Warehouses are essential operational facilities responsible for receiving, storing, protecting and distributing enterprise resources.

The Warehouse Business Object standardises warehouse governance, operational control, inventory visibility and logistics coordination while ensuring consistency across departments, business units and technology platforms.

---

# Why the Warehouse Business Object Exists

Organisations frequently operate multiple warehouses distributed across sites, regions and countries.

Without a governed Warehouse Business Object they commonly experience:

- fragmented warehouse records
- inconsistent warehouse operations
- inaccurate inventory locations
- duplicated inventory
- poor stock visibility
- inefficient warehouse utilisation
- delayed order fulfilment
- limited operational reporting
- ineffective logistics planning

The Warehouse Business Object addresses these challenges by providing one canonical enterprise model for warehouse governance.

# Scope of Responsibility

The Warehouse Business Object governs:

- warehouse identity
- warehouse classification
- warehouse locations
- storage capacity
- warehouse status
- inventory locations
- operational history
- warehouse performance
- warehouse utilisation

Inventory quantities remain governed by inventory transactions and related operational workflows.
