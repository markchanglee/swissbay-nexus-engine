# 01 — Executive Summary

---

# Overview

The Warehouse Business Object defines the governed representation of every physical, virtual and logical warehouse operated within the Swissbay Nexus platform.

Warehouses provide the enterprise capability for receiving, storing, protecting, issuing, transferring and tracking inventory, assets and operational materials.

The Warehouse Business Object serves as the authoritative source of truth for warehouse identity, storage capacity, operational status, inventory locations, stock movement and warehouse governance.

Unlike the Product Business Object, which governs what the organisation buys, manufactures or sells, the Warehouse Business Object governs where those resources are stored and how they move through the organisation.

---

# Business Purpose

The Warehouse Business Object establishes one governed representation of every warehouse operated by the organisation.

It standardises:

- warehouse identity
- warehouse classification
- storage locations
- warehouse capacity
- inventory locations
- operational status
- warehouse utilisation
- stock movements
- warehouse governance

Every warehouse follows one consistent governance model regardless of location or business unit.

---

# Business Importance

Warehouses are critical operational facilities supporting the movement of enterprise resources.

Examples include:

- Mining Stores
- Distribution Centres
- Manufacturing Warehouses
- Spare Parts Stores
- Pharmaceutical Warehouses
- Cold Storage Facilities
- Equipment Depots
- Vehicle Stores
- IT Equipment Stores
- Project Warehouses
- Bonded Warehouses
- Virtual Warehouses

Without governed warehouse management organisations often experience:

- lost inventory
- duplicate stock
- inaccurate stock levels
- inefficient warehouse layouts
- delayed fulfilment
- excess inventory
- stock shortages
- poor operational visibility

Swissbay resolves these challenges by providing one canonical Warehouse Business Object.
