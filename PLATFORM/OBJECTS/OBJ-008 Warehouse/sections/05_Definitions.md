# 05 — Definitions

---

# Overview

This document defines the official business terminology used throughout the Warehouse Business Object.

These definitions establish a common enterprise vocabulary ensuring that Warehouse Operations, Supply Chain, Logistics, Procurement, Finance, Artificial Intelligence and enterprise systems interpret warehouse information consistently.

These definitions are the authoritative terminology for warehouse governance within Swissbay Nexus.

---

# Core Definitions

## Warehouse

A governed physical, virtual or logical facility used to receive, store, protect, manage and distribute enterprise resources.

Every Warehouse is uniquely represented by one Warehouse Business Object.

---

## Warehouse Identifier

The permanent globally unique identifier assigned to every Warehouse.

Example

```
WH-000145
```

Warehouse Identifiers:

- never change
- are never reused
- uniquely identify a Warehouse

---

## Warehouse Name

The approved business name describing the warehouse.

Example

```
Johannesburg Central Distribution Centre
```

---

## Warehouse Category

The high-level business grouping used for governance and reporting.

Examples include:

- Distribution Centre
- Mining Store
- Manufacturing Warehouse
- Spare Parts Store
- Cold Storage
- Project Warehouse
- Virtual Warehouse

---

## Warehouse Type

A more detailed operational classification.

Examples include:

- Finished Goods
- Raw Materials
- Maintenance Stores
- Hazardous Materials
- Bonded Warehouse
- Transit Warehouse

---

## Warehouse Manager

The individual accountable for warehouse operations, governance and performance.

The Warehouse Manager is responsible for operational effectiveness but not necessarily inventory ownership.

---

## Storage Zone

A governed subdivision within a Warehouse.

Examples include:

- Receiving
- Bulk Storage
- Picking
- Packing
- Dispatch
- Quarantine
- Hazardous Storage

---

## Storage Location

The smallest governed storage position where inventory or assets are stored.

Examples include:

```
Zone A

↓

Aisle 04

↓

Rack 08

↓

Shelf 03

↓

Bin 12
```

---

## Warehouse Capacity

The maximum storage capability of a Warehouse.

Capacity may be measured by:

- pallet positions
- cubic metres
- floor space
- weight
- storage bins

---

## Warehouse Utilisation

The percentage of Warehouse Capacity currently in use.

High utilisation may reduce operational efficiency.

---

## Goods Receipt

The governed process of accepting inventory, materials or assets into the Warehouse.

Receipt initiates inventory visibility.

---

## Goods Issue

The governed process of releasing inventory or assets from the Warehouse.

Goods Issue supports:

- operations
- projects
- customers
- manufacturing

---

## Warehouse Transfer

The movement of inventory or assets between governed warehouses or storage locations.

Transfers maintain full traceability.

---

## Stock Adjustment

A governed correction applied to inventory balances following authorised investigation.

Every adjustment requires an audit record.

---

## Warehouse Audit

A formal review verifying:

- inventory accuracy
- operational compliance
- storage conditions
- governance adherence

Audits strengthen warehouse governance.

---

## Artificial Intelligence

Artificial Intelligence refers to governed capabilities that analyse warehouse information to generate predictions, recommendations and operational insights.

AI supports warehouse operations but never replaces operational governance.

# Terminology Rules

Throughout the Warehouse Business Object:

- Use **Warehouse Identifier** rather than Warehouse ID.
- Use **Warehouse Manager** for operational accountability.
- Use **Storage Location** for governed inventory positions.
- Use **Warehouse Lifecycle** for operational lifecycle references.
- Use **Goods Receipt** rather than Receiving.
- Use **Goods Issue** rather than Dispatch when referring to inventory movements.

---

# AI Interpretation

Artificial Intelligence must interpret Warehouse terminology according to this document.

Where ambiguity exists, these definitions take precedence over external terminology.

Changes to warehouse terminology require:

- Warehouse Operations Review
- Enterprise Architecture Review
- Governance Approval
- Version Update

This document establishes the official enterprise vocabulary for the Warehouse Business Object.
