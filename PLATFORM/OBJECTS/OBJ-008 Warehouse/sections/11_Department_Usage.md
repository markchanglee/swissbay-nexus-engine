# 11 — Department Usage

---

# Overview

The Warehouse Business Object is an enterprise capability that supports nearly every operational department within the organisation.

While Warehouse Operations governs the Warehouse Business Object, multiple business units contribute to inventory management, logistics, procurement, finance, asset management and fulfilment.

This document defines departmental responsibilities and governance boundaries for enterprise warehouse management.

---

# Purpose

The Department Usage Model exists to:

- define departmental responsibilities
- strengthen governance
- improve collaboration
- eliminate ownership ambiguity
- improve accountability
- support Artificial Intelligence
- enable enterprise-wide warehouse management

Warehouses are shared enterprise capabilities governed through clearly defined responsibilities.

---

# Department Participation

The following departments interact with the Warehouse Business Object:

- Executive Management
- Warehouse Operations
- Supply Chain
- Logistics
- Procurement
- Operations
- Asset Management
- Finance
- Information Technology
- Information Security
- Quality Assurance
- Legal
- Artificial Intelligence

Each department performs governed activities throughout the Warehouse Lifecycle.

## Responsibilities

Executive Management provides strategic oversight.

Activities include:

- approve warehouse investments
- review warehouse performance

- monitor operational efficiency
- review strategic logistics initiatives

### Primary Responsibilities

- Strategic Planning

Departments should:

- communicate operational changes

- support continuous improvement

# Department Usage Summary

The Department Usage Model defines how every organisational function contributes to enterprise warehouse management within Swissbay Nexus.
