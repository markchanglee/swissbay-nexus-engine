# 11 — Department Usage

---

# Overview

The Project Business Object is an enterprise capability that supports collaboration across multiple departments.

While the Project Management Office (PMO) governs project delivery, every department contributes according to its responsibilities and authority.

This document defines how each department interacts with the Project Business Object and establishes clear ownership boundaries throughout the project lifecycle.

---

# Purpose

The Department Usage Model exists to:

- define departmental responsibilities
- improve cross-functional collaboration
- strengthen governance
- eliminate ownership ambiguity
- improve accountability
- support Artificial Intelligence
- enable enterprise-wide project delivery

Projects are collaborative initiatives supported by the entire organisation.

---

# Department Participation

The following departments interact with the Project Business Object:

- Executive Management
- Project Management Office (PMO)
- Operations
- Finance
- Procurement
- Human Resources
- Engineering
- Information Technology
- Information Security
- Legal
- Sales
- Customer Success
- Artificial Intelligence

Each department performs governed activities within approved workflows.

---

# Executive Management

## Responsibilities

Executive Management provides strategic direction and governance.

Activities include:

- approve strategic initiatives
- approve major investments
- review project health
- participate in Stage Gate reviews
- approve project closure
- monitor portfolio performance

### Primary Responsibilities

- Strategic Alignment
- Executive Governance
- Portfolio Oversight

---

# Project Management Office (PMO)

## Responsibilities

The PMO owns the Project Business Object.

Activities include:

- define project methodology
- maintain governance standards
- approve lifecycle transitions
- monitor project performance
- support Project Managers
- manage portfolio reporting

### Primary Responsibilities

- Project Governance
- Standards
- Reporting
- Continuous Improvement

---

# Project Managers

## Responsibilities

Project Managers coordinate project execution.

Activities include:

- planning
- scheduling
- resource coordination
- stakeholder communication
- budget monitoring
- risk management
- issue management
- project reporting

Project Managers are accountable for delivery but not necessarily business ownership.

---

# Operations

## Responsibilities

Operations supports project execution by:

- providing operational resources
- coordinating implementation
- supporting deployment
- validating operational readiness
- accepting operational handover

Operations ensures successful transition into business-as-usual.

---

# Finance

## Responsibilities

Finance governs financial performance.

Activities include:

- approve budgets
- monitor expenditure

- forecast project costs
- validate financial reporting
- support benefit realisation

Finance owns financial governance rather than project execution.

# Collaboration Principles

Departments should:

- share accurate information
- follow governed workflows
- communicate proactively
- resolve issues collaboratively
- maintain accountability
- support project objectives

Projects succeed through coordinated enterprise collaboration.

---

# Department Governance

Department responsibilities require:

- defined ownership
- documented approval authority
- workflow participation
- audit logging
- role-based security

Ownership remains explicit throughout the project lifecycle.

---

# Department Usage Summary

The Department Usage Model defines how each organisational function contributes to successful project delivery within Swissbay Nexus.

By clearly assigning responsibilities across governance, finance, operations, technology, legal, procurement and customer engagement, the Project Business Object enables coordinated enterprise execution while maintaining accountability, transparency and consistent governance.
