# 06 — Asset Lifecycle

---

# Overview

The Asset Lifecycle defines the governed progression of every enterprise asset from acquisition through retirement and disposal.

Every Asset follows a standard lifecycle that ensures consistent governance, financial accountability, operational reliability and regulatory compliance.

An Asset exists in one—and only one—lifecycle stage at any given time.

---

# Purpose

The Asset Lifecycle exists to:

- standardise asset governance
- improve operational reliability
- strengthen financial control
- optimise maintenance
- support Artificial Intelligence
- improve lifecycle visibility
- maximise asset value

The lifecycle provides one operating model for enterprise assets.

---

# Lifecycle Overview

Every Asset progresses through the following stages.

```text
Planning

↓

Acquisition

↓

Commissioning

↓

Active Operation

↓

Maintenance

↓

Inspection

↓

Upgrade / Transfer

↓

Retirement

↓

Disposal

↓

Archived
```

Assets may move repeatedly between **Active Operation**, **Maintenance** and **Inspection** throughout their operational life.

---

# Stage 1 — Planning

## Purpose

Identify the need for a new asset.

### Activities

- business justification
- budget planning
- requirements definition
- supplier evaluation

### Exit Criteria

Asset acquisition approved.

---

# Stage 2 — Acquisition

## Purpose

Acquire the asset.

### Activities

- procurement
- supplier engagement
- contract execution
- delivery

### Outputs

Asset received and registered.

---

# Stage 3 — Commissioning

## Purpose

Prepare the asset for operational use.

### Activities

- installation
- testing
- certification
- configuration
- acceptance

### Outputs

Asset approved for operation.

---

# Stage 4 — Active Operation

## Purpose

Deliver operational value.

### Activities

- production
- operational usage
- utilisation monitoring
- performance tracking

This is normally the longest lifecycle stage.

---

# Stage 5 — Maintenance

## Purpose

Maintain operational reliability.

### Activities

- preventive maintenance
- corrective maintenance
- predictive maintenance
- repairs
- servicing

Maintenance preserves asset performance.

---

# Stage 6 — Inspection

## Purpose

Verify safety, compliance and operational condition.

### Activities

- inspections
- condition assessments
- calibration
- regulatory checks

Inspection outcomes may trigger maintenance or retirement.

---

# Stage 7 — Upgrade / Transfer

## Purpose

Improve or relocate the asset.

### Activities

- refurbishment
- upgrades
- reassignment
- location transfer
- ownership transfer

Asset identity remains unchanged.

---

# Stage 8 — Retirement

## Purpose

Remove the asset from operational service.

### Activities

- operational shutdown
- financial review
- replacement planning
- lifecycle completion

Retired assets remain governed.

---

# Stage 9 — Disposal

## Purpose

Permanently remove the asset.

### Activities

- resale
- recycling
- destruction
- donation
- disposal certification

Disposal completes the operational lifecycle.

---

# Stage 10 — Archived

## Purpose

Retain historical asset information.

Archived assets:

- remain searchable
- support audits
- support financial reporting
- support AI learning
- cannot be modified without governance approval

---

# Lifecycle Events

Major lifecycle events include:

- Asset Planned
- Asset Ordered
- Asset Received
- Asset Commissioned
- Asset Activated
- Maintenance Completed
- Inspection Passed
- Asset Upgraded
- Asset Retired
- Asset Disposed
- Asset Archived

These events trigger workflows, automation and dashboards.

---

# AI Throughout the Lifecycle

Artificial Intelligence may:

- predict maintenance
- estimate Remaining Useful Life
- forecast failures
- optimise maintenance schedules
- recommend replacement timing
- detect abnormal behaviour

AI remains advisory throughout the lifecycle.

---

# Governance

Every lifecycle transition requires:

- validation
- approval where applicable
- audit logging
- workflow execution
- Business Rule compliance

The Swissbay Governance Engine enforces lifecycle integrity.
