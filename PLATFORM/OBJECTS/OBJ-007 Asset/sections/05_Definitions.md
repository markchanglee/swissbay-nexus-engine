# 05 — Definitions

---

# Overview

This document defines the official business terminology used throughout the Asset Business Object.

These definitions establish a common enterprise vocabulary ensuring that Asset Managers, Finance, Engineering, Operations, Artificial Intelligence and enterprise systems interpret asset information consistently.

These definitions are the authoritative terminology for enterprise asset governance within Swissbay Nexus.

---

# Core Definitions

## Asset

A governed physical, digital or intangible resource owned, leased or controlled by the organisation that provides operational, financial or strategic value throughout its lifecycle.

Every Asset is uniquely represented by one Asset Business Object.

---

## Asset Identifier

The permanent globally unique identifier assigned to every Asset.

Example

```
AST-000451
```

Asset Identifiers:

- never change
- are never reused
- uniquely identify an Asset

---

## Asset Name

The approved business name describing the asset.

Example

```
CAT 785D Haul Truck
```

---

## Asset Category

The high-level grouping used for governance and reporting.

Examples include:

- Vehicle
- Equipment
- Building
- Infrastructure
- Software
- Network Device
- Machinery

---

## Asset Type

A more detailed classification within an Asset Category.

Example

```
Category: Vehicle

Type: Underground Loader
```

---

## Asset Owner

The department or business function accountable for the asset throughout its lifecycle.

Ownership represents accountability rather than physical possession.

---

## Asset Custodian

The individual or team responsible for the day-to-day care and operation of the asset.

Custodians may change during the asset lifecycle.

---

## Asset Status

Represents the current operational condition of the asset.

Typical values include:

- Planned
- Ordered
- Commissioning
- Active
- Under Maintenance
- Suspended
- Retired
- Disposed

---

## Asset Location

The governed physical or logical location of the asset.

Examples include:

- Mine Site
- Warehouse
- Office
- Plant
- Cloud Environment
- Data Centre

---

## Asset Condition

An engineering assessment of the asset's operational state.

Examples include:

- Excellent
- Good
- Fair
- Poor
- Critical

---

## Asset Value

The financial value assigned to the asset according to approved accounting policies.

Examples include:

- Acquisition Cost
- Current Book Value
- Replacement Value
- Residual Value

---

## Maintenance

Any planned or unplanned activity undertaken to preserve or restore the asset.

Maintenance includes:

- Preventive Maintenance
- Corrective Maintenance
- Predictive Maintenance
- Emergency Repairs

---

## Inspection

A scheduled assessment performed to verify condition, safety or compliance.

Inspections may trigger maintenance activities.

---

## Depreciation

The systematic reduction in the accounting value of an asset over its useful life.

Depreciation follows approved financial standards.

---

## Warranty

The contractual agreement defining supplier obligations for repair or replacement during a specified period.

---

## Remaining Useful Life (RUL)

The estimated operational lifespan remaining before replacement or retirement is recommended.

AI may assist in estimating Remaining Useful Life.

---

## Retirement

The formal removal of an asset from operational use.

Retirement does not necessarily imply disposal.

---

## Disposal

The final transfer, sale, recycling or destruction of an asset following retirement.

Disposal permanently ends the asset lifecycle.

---

## Artificial Intelligence

Artificial Intelligence refers to governed capabilities that analyse asset information to generate predictions, recommendations and insights.

AI supports asset management but never replaces engineering or financial governance.

# Terminology Rules

Throughout the Asset Business Object:

- Use **Asset Identifier** rather than Asset ID.
- Use **Asset Owner** for business accountability.
- Use **Asset Custodian** for operational responsibility.
- Use **Asset Lifecycle** for lifecycle references.
- Use **Maintenance** rather than Repair unless referring to corrective work.
- Use **Disposal** only for the final lifecycle stage.

---

# AI Interpretation

Artificial Intelligence must interpret Asset terminology according to this document.

Where ambiguity exists, these definitions take precedence over external terminology.
