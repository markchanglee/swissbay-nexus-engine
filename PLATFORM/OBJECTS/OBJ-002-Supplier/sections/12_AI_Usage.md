# 12 — AI Usage

---

# Overview

Artificial Intelligence is a governed participant within the Supplier Business Object.

Rather than replacing procurement professionals, AI augments supplier management by analysing information, identifying patterns, generating recommendations and supporting informed decision-making.

Every AI capability operates within the boundaries defined by the Swissbay AI Operating Standard (AI-000), Security Standard (SEC-000) and Governance Standard.

AI is a business assistant—not a business owner.

---

# Purpose

The AI Usage Model exists to:

- improve procurement efficiency
- reduce supplier risk
- improve data quality
- support supplier governance
- provide intelligent recommendations
- automate repetitive analysis
- improve executive decision-making

AI always supports human judgement.

---

# AI Responsibilities

Artificial Intelligence may assist throughout the supplier lifecycle.

Primary responsibilities include:

- supplier analysis
- supplier summarisation
- duplicate detection
- procurement recommendations
- supplier risk assessment
- compliance monitoring
- performance analysis
- procurement forecasting

# AI During the Supplier Lifecycle

## Prospective

AI may:

- recommend potential suppliers
- analyse market information
- identify strategic sourcing opportunities

---

## Registration

AI may:

- validate captured information
- detect duplicate suppliers
- recommend missing information
- classify supplier categories

---

## Qualification

AI may:

- analyse financial indicators
- evaluate submitted documentation
- assess compliance completeness
- calculate preliminary supplier risk

---

## Approval

AI may:

- generate approval recommendations
- explain identified risks
- summarise qualification findings

AI may not approve suppliers.

---

## Active

AI continuously monitors:

- supplier performance
- delivery reliability
- compliance status
- procurement trends
- pricing behaviour
- supplier responsiveness

---

## Performance Review

AI may:

- calculate performance trends
- identify declining suppliers
- recommend improvement actions
- benchmark suppliers

---

## Suspension

AI may identify:

- unusual procurement behaviour
- compliance failures
- declining performance
- contractual anomalies

Recommendations require procurement review.

---

## Offboarding

AI may:

- summarise supplier history
- capture lessons learned
- identify replacement suppliers
- generate executive reports

# Explainability

Every significant AI recommendation should include:

- recommendation
- confidence score
- supporting Business Objects
- supporting KPIs
- supporting Business Rules
- identified assumptions
- alternative options

Recommendations should always be explainable.

---

# Confidence Levels

Swissbay classifies AI confidence using the following model.

| Confidence | Meaning |
|------------|---------|
| 95–100% | Very High |
| 85–94% | High |
| 70–84% | Moderate |
| Below 70% | Low |

Low-confidence recommendations should require additional review.

---

# AI Restrictions

Artificial Intelligence may not:

- approve suppliers
- change supplier ownership
- alter lifecycle stages
- bypass workflows
- override Business Rules
- modify supplier records directly
- expose confidential supplier information

Human accountability is always preserved.

---

# AI Security

AI must:

- inherit user permissions
- respect security classifications
- record significant recommendations
- protect confidential information
- maintain audit history

AI security follows SEC-000.

---

# AI Governance

Every AI capability requires:

- Business Owner
- Technical Owner
- Data Sources
- Review Frequency
- Monitoring
- Approval

AI capabilities remain governed assets.
