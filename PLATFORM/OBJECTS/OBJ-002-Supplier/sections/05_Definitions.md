# 05 — Definitions

---

# Overview

This document defines the official business terminology used by the Supplier Business Object.

These definitions establish a common language for Procurement, Finance, Operations, Legal, Executive Management and Artificial Intelligence.

Every stakeholder should interpret these terms consistently regardless of department, system or geography.

The definitions contained within this document are considered the authoritative terminology for supplier management across Swissbay Nexus.

---

# Core Definitions

## Supplier

A Supplier is any organisation or individual that provides goods, services or strategic capabilities to Swissbay under an agreed commercial relationship.

A Supplier may provide:

- Products
- Services
- Equipment
- Professional Services
- Raw Materials
- Logistics
- Technology
- Consulting

Every Supplier is uniquely represented by one Supplier Business Object.

---

## Vendor

Within Swissbay Nexus, the terms **Vendor** and **Supplier** are treated as equivalent.

The preferred business term is **Supplier**.

The term Vendor may appear only when required by external systems or legislation.

---

## Supplier Identifier

The permanent, unique identifier assigned to every Supplier.

Example

```
SUPPLIER-000001
```

Supplier Identifiers:

- never change
- are never reused
- uniquely identify a Supplier across the platform

---

## Preferred Supplier

A Supplier that has been formally approved as a preferred source for one or more categories of goods or services.

Preferred Suppliers typically demonstrate:

- competitive pricing
- high quality
- reliable delivery
- regulatory compliance
- strong commercial relationships

---

## Strategic Supplier

A Supplier whose products or services are critical to Swissbay's long-term operations or competitive advantage.

Loss of a Strategic Supplier could significantly impact business continuity.

---

## Supplier Category

A classification describing the primary type of goods or services supplied.

Examples include:

- Technology
- Logistics
- Mining Equipment
- Construction
- Professional Services
- Manufacturing
- Healthcare
- Office Supplies

---

## Supplier Status

The current lifecycle status of a Supplier.

Approved values include:

- Draft
- Pending Approval
- Approved
- Active
- Suspended
- Inactive
- Archived

---

## Supplier Qualification

The process of evaluating whether a Supplier satisfies Swissbay's commercial, financial, operational and compliance requirements before approval.

Qualification may include:

- financial assessment
- capability review
- reference checks
- compliance verification
- technical evaluation

---

## Supplier Approval

The formal governance process that authorises a Supplier to conduct business with Swissbay.

Approval is performed by authorised Procurement personnel.

Artificial Intelligence may assist the evaluation process but may not grant approval.

---

## Supplier Performance

The measurable assessment of a Supplier's effectiveness over time.

Performance may consider:

- delivery reliability
- quality
- responsiveness
- pricing
- compliance
- customer satisfaction
- contract adherence

---

## Supplier Risk

The likelihood that a Supplier could negatively affect Swissbay's operations, finances, reputation or compliance obligations.

Risk categories may include:

- financial
- operational
- legal
- cybersecurity
- environmental
- geopolitical
- reputational

---

## Supplier Relationship

The governed commercial relationship between Swissbay and a Supplier.

Relationships may include:

- strategic partnership
- preferred supplier
- approved supplier
- temporary supplier
- one-time supplier

---

## Contract

A legally binding agreement between Swissbay and a Supplier.

Contracts are governed by the Contract Business Object.

The Supplier Business Object references Contracts but does not own them.

---

## Procurement

The business function responsible for sourcing, evaluating, negotiating and managing suppliers.

Procurement owns the Supplier Business Object.

---

## Compliance

The degree to which a Supplier satisfies applicable legal, regulatory, contractual and organisational requirements.

Examples include:

- ISO Certifications
- BBBEE
- Tax Compliance
- Environmental Regulations
- Health and Safety Requirements

---

## Artificial Intelligence

Artificial Intelligence refers to governed AI capabilities that assist procurement by analysing supplier information, generating recommendations and identifying potential risks.

AI remains advisory and does not replace procurement governance.

---

## Business Owner

The individual or department accountable for the business meaning, governance and strategic direction of the Supplier Business Object.

For this object:

**Business Owner: Procurement**

# Terminology Rules

The following terminology rules apply throughout this Business Object.

- Use **Supplier** instead of Vendor wherever possible.
- Use **Business Object** rather than Record.
- Use **Business Owner** rather than System Owner.
- Use **Business Rule** rather than Validation Rule when referring to business behaviour.
- Use **Relationship** rather than Link when describing Business Object connections.

Consistent terminology improves governance, reporting and AI interpretation.

---

# AI Interpretation

Artificial Intelligence should interpret every defined term according to this document.

AI should not substitute alternative business meanings unless explicitly defined by another governed standard.

Where ambiguity exists, this document takes precedence for Supplier terminology.
