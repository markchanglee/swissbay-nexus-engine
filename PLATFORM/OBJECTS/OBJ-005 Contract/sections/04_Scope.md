# 04 — Scope

---

# Overview

The Contract Business Object defines the governance boundaries for enterprise agreement management within Swissbay Nexus.

Its purpose is to clearly identify which responsibilities belong to the Contract Business Object and which remain the responsibility of other Business Objects.

Clearly defined scope eliminates duplication, improves interoperability and strengthens enterprise governance.

The Contract Business Object governs agreements—not every document or downstream business process.

---

# Purpose

The Scope Model exists to:

- define ownership boundaries
- establish enterprise responsibilities
- reduce duplication
- improve interoperability
- strengthen governance
- support Artificial Intelligence
- simplify integration

The Contract Business Object represents the canonical agreement for the enterprise.

---

# Guiding Principles

The Contract Business Object is responsible for governing:

- legal agreements
- commercial commitments
- financial commitments
- operational obligations
- lifecycle management
- contractual governance

It does **not** replace document management systems, ERP systems or project management platforms.

---

# In Scope

The Contract Business Object governs:

- Contract Identity
- Contract Type
- Contract Parties
- Commercial Terms
- Legal Terms
- Financial Terms
- Pricing Structure
- Deliverables
- Obligations
- Milestones
- Effective Dates
- Expiry Dates
- Renewal Rules
- Amendments
- Lifecycle Status
- Compliance Status
- Audit History

These elements form the canonical representation of every enterprise Contract.

---

# Identity Scope

The Contract Business Object owns:

- Contract Identifier
- Contract Number
- Contract Name
- Contract Classification
- Contract Status
- Version

Identity remains permanent throughout the Contract Lifecycle.

---

# Party Management Scope

The Contract Business Object governs:

- contracting parties
- customer relationships
- supplier relationships
- legal entities
- signatories
- contract owners

Identity for Customers, Suppliers and Employees remains governed by their own Business Objects.

---

# Commercial Scope

The Contract Business Object governs:

- commercial commitments
- pricing models
- payment schedules
- deliverables
- acceptance criteria
- renewal options

Commercial execution continues through Projects and Sales Orders.

---

# Legal Scope

The Contract Business Object governs:

- legal clauses
- governing law
- jurisdiction
- liability
- indemnities
- intellectual property
- confidentiality
- termination conditions

Legal interpretation remains the responsibility of authorised legal professionals.

---

# Operational Scope

The Contract Business Object governs:

- contractual obligations
- milestones
- service levels
- deliverable commitments
- operational dependencies

Operational execution is governed by Projects, Sales Orders and Operations.

---

# Financial Scope

The Contract Business Object governs:

- contract value
- payment terms
- billing milestones
- pricing schedules
- commercial commitments

Financial transactions remain governed by Finance systems.

---

# Lifecycle Scope

The Contract Business Object governs:

- Draft
- Review
- Negotiation
- Approval
- Signature
- Active
- Amendment
- Renewal
- Expiry
- Archival

Lifecycle governance belongs exclusively to the Contract Business Object.

---

# AI Scope

Artificial Intelligence may:

- classify clauses
- compare contracts
- identify risks
- predict renewals
- monitor obligations
- generate summaries
- recommend improvements

AI may not:

- approve contracts
- sign agreements
- modify legal clauses
- override governance
- execute legal decisions

Human accountability remains mandatory.

---

# Out of Scope

The Contract Business Object does **not** govern:

- Customer Master Data
- Supplier Master Data
- Employee Records
- Product Master Data
- Project Delivery
- Warehouse Operations
- Inventory
- Financial Accounting
- Invoice Processing
- Payment Execution
- HR Agreements outside approved scope

These capabilities remain governed by their respective Business Objects.

---

# Relationship Scope

The Contract Business Object references:

- Customer
- Supplier
- Opportunity
- Product
- Employee
- Project
- Sales Order
- Asset
- Warehouse

Each related Business Object retains ownership of its own identity and lifecycle.

---

# Future Scope

Future versions may expand to include:

- Digital Signatures
- Smart Contracts
- Blockchain Verification
- ESG Compliance
- AI Negotiation
- Multi-language Clause Libraries
- Regulatory Intelligence

Future enhancements must preserve backwards compatibility.

---

# Scope Governance

Changes to Contract scope require:

- Legal Review
- Commercial Review
- Enterprise Architecture Review
- Governance Board Approval
- Version Update

Scope changes should remain intentional and fully documented.

---

# Scope Summary

The Contract Business Object governs the complete representation of enterprise agreements while coordinating related Business Objects through governed relationships.

Its scope is intentionally limited to contract governance, ensuring clear ownership boundaries, strong interoperability and a maintainable enterprise architecture.
