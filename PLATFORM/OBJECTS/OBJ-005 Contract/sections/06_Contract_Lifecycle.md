# 06 — Contract Lifecycle

---

# Overview

The Contract Lifecycle defines the governed progression of every Contract from initial drafting through archival.

Every Contract follows a standard lifecycle that ensures legal governance, commercial consistency, operational readiness, regulatory compliance and enterprise visibility.

A Contract exists in one—and only one—lifecycle stage at any given time.

Lifecycle transitions are governed through Business Rules, Validation, Security and Workflow policies.

---

# Purpose

The Contract Lifecycle exists to:

- standardise agreement management
- strengthen legal governance
- improve commercial consistency
- reduce contractual risk
- support Artificial Intelligence
- improve compliance
- maximise operational readiness

The lifecycle provides one enterprise operating model for all Contracts.

---

---

# Stage 1 — Draft

## Purpose

Create the initial Contract.

### Activities

- assign Contract Identifier
- assign Contract Owner
- define Contract Type
- identify Contract Parties
- capture commercial information

### Outputs

Initial governed Contract.

### Exit Criteria

Mandatory information completed.

---

# Stage 2 — Internal Review

## Purpose

Validate business readiness.

### Activities

- verify commercial requirements
- review pricing
- validate deliverables
- confirm stakeholders

### Outputs

Business-approved draft.

---

# Stage 3 — Legal Review

## Purpose

Review legal enforceability.

### Activities

- clause review
- governing law
- liability review
- indemnities
- intellectual property
- confidentiality

### Outputs

Legally approved Contract.

---

# Stage 4 — Commercial Review

## Purpose

Validate commercial viability.

### Activities

- pricing approval
- financial review
- revenue validation
- procurement review
- executive approval where required

### Outputs

Commercially approved agreement.

---

# Stage 5 — Negotiation

## Purpose

Reach agreement between Contract Parties.

### Activities

- negotiate clauses
- negotiate pricing
- review amendments
- capture negotiation history

### Outputs

Final negotiated Contract.

---

# Stage 6 — Approved

## Purpose

Prepare the Contract for execution.

### Activities

- final validation
- approval recording
- signature preparation

### Outputs

Ready for signature.

---

# Stage 7 — Signed

## Purpose

Legally execute the agreement.

### Activities

- collect signatures
- record execution date
- publish signed version
- lock approved clauses

### Outputs

Legally enforceable Contract.

---

# Stage 8 — Active

## Purpose

Manage the operational agreement.

### Activities

- monitor obligations
- monitor milestones
- monitor payments
- monitor service levels
- monitor compliance

### Outputs

Operational Contract.

---

# Stage 9 — Obligation Management

## Purpose

Track contractual commitments throughout execution.

### Activities

- monitor obligations
- notify owners
- monitor due dates
- monitor deliverables
- monitor service levels

Obligations remain linked to the governing Contract.

---

# Stage 10 — Renewal Review

## Purpose

Determine future Contract direction.

### Activities

- evaluate performance
- assess renewal options
- perform commercial review
- perform legal review
- negotiate renewal

### Outputs

Renewal recommendation.

---

# Stage 11A — Renewed

Activities include:

- create new Contract version
- preserve history
- update effective dates
- restart lifecycle

Previous Contract versions remain immutable.

---

# Stage 11B — Expired

Activities include:

- close obligations
- archive commitments
- notify stakeholders
- preserve history

Expired Contracts remain searchable.

---

# Stage 12 — Archived

Archived Contracts:

- remain searchable
- support reporting
- support AI learning
- support audit requirements
- cannot be modified without governance approval

Historical Contracts remain permanent enterprise records.

---

# Lifecycle Events

Major lifecycle events include:

- Contract Created
- Legal Review Completed
- Commercial Approval Granted
- Negotiation Completed
- Contract Signed
- Contract Activated
- Obligation Due
- Amendment Approved
- Renewal Initiated
- Contract Renewed
- Contract Expired
- Contract Archived

These events trigger workflows, automation and notifications.

---

# Contract Amendments

Amendments do not replace Contracts.

Instead:

```text
Contract

├── Amendment 1
├── Amendment 2
├── Amendment 3
```

Every amendment preserves complete historical traceability.

---

# Contract Package

Throughout the lifecycle every Contract references its Contract Package.

```text
Contract Package

├── Master Agreement
├── SOW
├── SLA
├── Pricing Schedule
├── Annexures
├── Insurance
├── Amendments
└── Signatures
```

The Contract remains the governing object.
