# 06 — Employee Lifecycle

---

# Overview

The Employee Lifecycle defines the complete journey of every authorised worker within the Swissbay Nexus platform.

Beginning with recruitment and ending with archival of employment records, the lifecycle ensures that every Employee progresses through governed stages with full visibility, accountability and auditability.

Each Employee exists in one—and only one—lifecycle stage at any given time.

---

# Purpose

The Employee Lifecycle exists to:

- standardise workforce management
- improve governance
- strengthen security
- improve onboarding
- support compliance
- enable automation
- enable Artificial Intelligence
- maintain complete workforce history

The lifecycle provides one consistent operating model across the enterprise.

---

# Lifecycle Overview

Every Employee progresses through the following stages.

```text
Candidate

↓

Offer Extended

↓

Pre-Onboarding

↓

Onboarding

↓

Active

↓

Career Development

↓

Transfer / Promotion

↓

↓

↓
```



---

# Stage 1 — Candidate

## Purpose

A prospective employee exists within the recruitment process.

### Activities

- application received
- CV reviewed
- interviews completed
- background checks initiated

### Exit Criteria

Offer approved.

---

# Stage 2 — Offer Extended

## Purpose

The organisation has formally offered employment.

### Activities

- employment offer generated
- salary confirmed
- employment conditions agreed
- acceptance awaited

### Exit Criteria

Offer accepted.

Alternative

Offer declined.

---

# Stage 3 — Pre-Onboarding

## Purpose

Preparation before the employee's first working day.

### Activities

- employee record created
- Employee Identifier generated
- IT equipment requested
- access requests initiated
- manager assigned
- onboarding plan created

### Exit Criteria

Employment commencement date reached.

---

# Stage 4 — Onboarding

## Purpose

The employee joins the organisation.

### Activities

- identity verified
- employment activated
- induction completed
- system access granted
- mandatory training assigned

### Outputs

Active Employee.

---

# Stage 5 — Active

## Purpose

The employee performs authorised work.

### Activities

- workflow participation
- approvals
- task execution
- business ownership
- KPI contribution
- training
- performance reviews

This is the primary operational stage.

---

# Stage 6 — Career Development

## Purpose

Professional growth and capability development.

### Activities

- training
- certification
- mentoring
- competency reviews
- skills development
- succession planning

AI may recommend development opportunities.

---

# Stage 7 — Transfer / Promotion

## Purpose

The employee changes organisational responsibilities.

### Activities

- department transfer
- manager change
- promotion
- role update
- responsibility reassignment

Historical records remain preserved.

---

# Stage 8 — Leave

## Purpose

The employee is temporarily unavailable.

Examples include:

- annual leave
- sick leave
- maternity leave
- study leave
- unpaid leave

The Employee remains Active but unavailable.

---

# Stage 9 — Return to Work

## Purpose

The employee resumes normal duties.

### Activities

- availability restored
- workflows reactivated
- approvals reinstated
- capacity updated

---

# Stage 10 — Offboarding

## Purpose

Employment concludes.

### Activities

- access revoked
- assets returned
- final approvals completed
- knowledge transfer
- exit interview
- payroll finalisation

The Employee record remains governed.

---

# Stage 11 — Archived

## Purpose

Historical employment records are retained.

Archived Employees:

- remain searchable
- support audits
- support reporting
- cannot perform work
- cannot authenticate

Retention follows legal and organisational policy.

---

# Exception Path — Application Rejected

## Purpose

Candidate does not proceed to employment.

### Activities

- recruitment closed
- recruitment records retained
- candidate notified

---

# Exception Path — Offer Declined

## Purpose

Candidate declines employment.

### Activities

- offer withdrawn
- recruitment records updated
- position reopened if required

| Pre-Onboarding | Onboarding | HR |

| Active | Transfer / Promotion | HR / Manager |

# Lifecycle Summary

The Employee Lifecycle provides the governed framework for managing every authorised worker from recruitment through archival.

By defining controlled lifecycle stages, approvals, organisational changes, workforce development and AI-assisted workforce planning, Swissbay Nexus ensures that every Employee remains a trusted enterprise identity supporting governance, security, accountability and operational excellence throughout the organisation.
