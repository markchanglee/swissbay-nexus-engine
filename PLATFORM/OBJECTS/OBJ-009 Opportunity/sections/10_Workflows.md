# 10 — Workflows

---

# Overview

The Workflow Model defines the governed business processes that manage Contracts throughout their lifecycle.

Workflows coordinate drafting, legal review, commercial approvals, negotiation, execution, obligation management, amendments, renewals and archival while ensuring compliance with Business Rules, Security policies and enterprise governance.

Every workflow is event-driven, fully auditable and technology independent.

---

# Purpose

The Contract Workflow Model exists to:

- standardise contract execution
- strengthen governance
- improve legal consistency
- automate approvals
- improve collaboration
- support Artificial Intelligence
- reduce contractual risk
- improve operational accountability

Workflows transform contract governance into repeatable enterprise processes.

---

# Workflow Principles

Contract workflows follow these principles:

- business-first
- event-driven
- governed
- auditable
- secure
- reusable
- explainable

Workflows describe business behaviour rather than software implementation.

# WF-CON-001 — Contract Registration

## Trigger

Approved commercial requirement.

## Workflow

1. Create Contract
2. Assign Contract Identifier
3. Assign Contract Owner
4. Register Contract Parties
5. Create Contract Package
6. Record audit history

---

# WF-CON-002 — Internal Review

## Trigger

Draft Completed

## Workflow

1. Validate business information
2. Review pricing
3. Validate deliverables
4. Confirm stakeholders
5. Progress lifecycle

---

# WF-CON-003 — Legal Review

## Trigger

Internal Review Approved

## Workflow

1. Review clauses
2. Validate governing law
3. Assess liability
4. Review confidentiality
5. Record legal approval

---

# WF-CON-004 — Commercial Review

## Trigger

Legal Review Completed

## Workflow

1. Validate pricing
2. Review commercial terms
3. Validate financial commitments
4. Obtain approvals
5. Progress lifecycle

---

# WF-CON-005 — Negotiation

## Trigger

Commercial Review Completed

## Workflow

1. Exchange revisions
2. Record negotiation history
3. Review amendments
4. Update Contract
5. Obtain final agreement

---

# WF-CON-006 — Contract Execution

## Trigger

Final Approval Granted

## Workflow

1. Collect signatures
2. Record execution date
3. Publish signed version
4. Activate Contract
5. Notify stakeholders

---

# WF-CON-007 — Obligation Management

## Trigger

Contract Activated

## Workflow

1. Create obligations
2. Assign owners
3. Monitor milestones
4. Monitor due dates
5. Escalate overdue obligations

---

# WF-CON-008 — Amendment Management

## Trigger

Approved Change Request

## Workflow

1. Create Amendment
2. Validate legal impact
3. Obtain approvals
4. Update Contract history
5. Publish Amendment

---

# WF-CON-009 — Renewal Management

## Trigger

Renewal Window Reached

## Workflow

1. Review performance
2. Assess renewal
3. Review pricing
4. Negotiate changes
5. Renew or expire Contract

---

# WF-CON-010 — Contract Closure

## Trigger

Contract Expired or Terminated

## Workflow

1. Verify obligations complete
2. Close milestones
3. Archive Contract
4. Preserve audit history
5. Notify stakeholders

# Workflow Events

Major workflow events include:

- Contract Created
- Review Completed
- Legal Approval Granted
- Commercial Approval Granted
- Negotiation Completed
- Contract Signed
- Contract Activated
- Obligation Due
- Amendment Approved
- Renewal Initiated
- Contract Renewed
- Contract Expired
- Contract Archived

These events trigger automation, dashboards and enterprise integrations.
