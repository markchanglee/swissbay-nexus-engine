# 06 — Product Lifecycle

---

# Overview

The Product Lifecycle defines the complete business journey of a Product within the Swissbay Nexus platform.

Products evolve from an initial concept through planning, approval, operational use and eventual retirement.

The lifecycle provides a governed framework that ensures products remain accurate, traceable and reusable throughout their existence while supporting Procurement, Operations, Warehousing, Sales, Finance and Artificial Intelligence.

Every Product exists in one—and only one—lifecycle stage at any given time.

---

# Purpose

The Product Lifecycle exists to:

- standardise product governance
- improve product quality
- reduce operational risk
- improve inventory planning
- support automation
- improve reporting
- enable AI
- support enterprise scalability

The lifecycle provides consistent management of Products from creation to retirement.

---

# Lifecycle Overview

Every Product progresses through the following stages.

```text
Concept

↓

Draft

↓

Review

↓

Approved

↓

Active

↓

Available for Sale (Optional)

↓

Maintained

↓

Discontinued

↓

Retired

↓

Archived
```

Each transition is governed by Business Rules and approval requirements.

---

# Stage 1 — Concept

## Purpose

A business need for a new Product has been identified.

Examples include:

- customer demand
- supplier proposal
- new service
- market opportunity
- product innovation
- regulatory requirement

### Activities

- business case
- market analysis
- product proposal
- initial costing

### Exit Criteria

Approved to proceed to Draft.

---

# Stage 2 — Draft

## Purpose

The Product Business Object is created.

Basic product information is captured.

### Required Information

- Product Code
- Product Name
- Product Category
- Product Type
- Unit of Measure
- Business Owner

### Activities

- define characteristics
- assign category
- assign owner
- define relationships

### Exit Criteria

Ready for Review.

---

# Stage 3 — Review

## Purpose

Business stakeholders review the Product before approval.

Review participants may include:

- Operations
- Procurement
- Warehouse
- Sales
- Finance
- Product Management

### AI Assistance

AI may:

- identify duplicate products
- recommend classifications
- validate descriptions
- suggest related products

### Exit Criteria

Product approved for operational use.

---

# Stage 4 — Approved

## Purpose

The Product satisfies governance requirements.

### Approval Requirements

- complete information
- valid classification
- required approvals
- validation passed

### AI Restrictions

Artificial Intelligence may recommend approval but may never approve Products.

### Exit Criteria

Product becomes Active.

---

# Stage 5 — Active

## Purpose

The Product becomes available for operational use.

The Product may now be:

- purchased
- stocked
- manufactured
- sold
- consumed
- referenced

This is the primary operational state.

---

# Stage 6 — Available for Sale (Optional)

## Purpose

The Product has been released for commercial sale.

Not every Product reaches this stage.

Examples that may not:

- internal services
- maintenance items
- consumables
- internal assets

### Activities

- sales catalogue
- pricing
- customer visibility
- commerce integrations

---

# Stage 7 — Maintained

## Purpose

The Product remains active but undergoes controlled changes.

Examples include:

- specification updates
- packaging changes
- supplier changes
- pricing reviews
- documentation updates

Business identity remains unchanged.

---

# Stage 8 — Discontinued

## Purpose

The Product is no longer offered for new procurement or sale.

Existing commitments may continue.

Typical reasons include:

- supplier discontinuation
- replacement product
- strategic decision
- regulatory changes
- poor performance

### Activities

- customer notification
- replacement planning
- inventory depletion

---

# Stage 9 — Retired

## Purpose

The Product is permanently removed from operational use.

### Activities

- remove from catalogues
- terminate procurement
- close relationships
- preserve history

Historical transactions remain unaffected.

---

# Stage 10 — Archived

## Purpose

The Product remains available for reporting, auditing and historical analysis.

Archived Products:

- cannot be purchased
- cannot be sold
- cannot be modified
- remain searchable
- preserve complete history

Retention follows governance requirements.

# Lifecycle Summary

The Product Lifecycle provides the governed operational framework for managing Products from concept through retirement.

By defining controlled lifecycle stages, approvals, validations and AI-supported decision making, Swissbay Nexus ensures that Products remain trusted enterprise assets capable of supporting procurement, warehousing, sales, finance, automation and Artificial Intelligence throughout their entire lifecycle.
