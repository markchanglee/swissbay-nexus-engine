# 11 — Department Usage

---

# Overview

The Product Business Object is a shared enterprise asset used throughout the Swissbay Nexus platform.

Although Product Management and Operations own the Product Business Object, many departments contribute to, govern and consume Product information during its lifecycle.

This document defines how each department interacts with the Product Business Object and clarifies ownership, responsibilities and expected business outcomes.

---

# Purpose

The Department Usage Model exists to:

- define departmental responsibilities
- establish ownership
- prevent duplicated effort
- improve collaboration
- support governance
- clarify accountability
- improve AI understanding of organisational roles

Every department should understand its responsibilities regarding Product information.

# Product Management

## Role

Product Management is the Business Owner of the Product Business Object.

### Responsibilities

- Create Products
- Maintain Product Information
- Classify Products
- Govern Product Lifecycles
- Coordinate Product Reviews
- Retire Products

### Business Value

Product Management gains:

- one governed product catalogue
- product visibility
- lifecycle governance
- enterprise consistency
- improved decision-making

---

# Operations

## Role

Operations governs the operational readiness of Products.

### Responsibilities

- approve Products
- maintain operational specifications
- coordinate lifecycle changes
- manage product standards
- oversee product governance

### Business Value

Operations benefits from:

- trusted product information
- standardised processes
- operational consistency
- improved planning

---

# Procurement

## Role

Procurement associates Products with Suppliers.

### Responsibilities

- link approved suppliers
- support sourcing
- maintain procurement classifications
- participate in product reviews

### Business Value

Procurement benefits from:

- trusted supplier-product relationships
- improved sourcing decisions
- procurement consistency

Procurement does not own Product information.

---

# Warehouse

## Role

Warehouse consumes Product information for inventory operations.

### Responsibilities

- define storage requirements
- manage inventory attributes
- support replenishment
- provide operational feedback

### Business Value

Warehouse gains:

- improved inventory accuracy
- simplified receiving
- improved picking
- improved stock visibility

Inventory ownership belongs to the Warehouse Business Object.

---

# Sales

## Role

Sales uses Products to create commercial opportunities and Sales Orders.

### Responsibilities

- maintain product availability awareness
- support pricing reviews
- provide customer feedback
- recommend product improvements

### Business Value

Sales benefits from:

- governed product information
- consistent product descriptions
- improved customer experience
- improved quoting

Sales does not own Product definitions.

---

# Finance

## Role

Finance consumes Product information for commercial and financial analysis.

### Responsibilities

- review costing
- review pricing
- perform profitability analysis
- support budgeting
- support financial reporting

### Business Value

Finance gains:

- consistent product costing
- reliable profitability analysis
- improved financial reporting

Historical pricing remains a Finance responsibility.

---

# Executive Management

## Role

Executives use Product information for strategic planning.

### Responsibilities

- review product portfolio
- monitor profitability
- evaluate product strategy
- approve strategic product decisions

### Business Value

Executives receive:

- product dashboards
- profitability insights
- lifecycle visibility
- AI recommendations

---

# Information Technology

## Role

Technology enables but does not own Product information.

### Responsibilities

- maintain integrations
- support platform availability
- manage APIs
- maintain technical infrastructure

### Business Value

IT benefits from:

- standard APIs
- reusable architecture
- governed integrations
- simplified support

---

# Artificial Intelligence

## Role

Artificial Intelligence operates as an advisory capability.

### Responsibilities

AI may:

- classify Products
- forecast demand
- identify duplicate Products
- recommend substitute Products
- optimise inventory
- analyse profitability
- generate summaries

AI may not:

- approve Products
- change Product ownership
- alter Product lifecycle
- bypass governance

---

# External Stakeholders

Approved external stakeholders may interact with Product information through governed integrations.

Examples include:

- ERP Systems
- eCommerce Platforms
- Supplier Portals
- Customer Portals
- Marketplace Platforms
- Manufacturing Systems

Access remains governed by Swissbay security standards.

# Collaboration

Departments collaborate through governed workflows including:

- Product Creation
- Product Review
- Product Approval
- Product Release
- Product Maintenance
- Product Retirement

The Product Business Object provides the shared information required for collaboration.

---

# Security Responsibilities

Every department is responsible for:

- protecting Product information
- complying with access controls
- maintaining data quality
- following governance
- reporting security concerns

Security responsibilities are shared across the enterprise.

---

# AI Perspective

Artificial Intelligence uses the Department Usage Model to determine:

- business ownership
- approval authority
- notification recipients
- governance responsibilities
- collaboration patterns

This improves AI explainability and governance.

---

# Department Usage Summary

The Product Business Object enables every major business function within Swissbay Nexus to operate from a single governed source of Product information.

By clearly defining departmental responsibilities, ownership and collaboration, Swissbay strengthens governance, improves operational efficiency and enables Artificial Intelligence and automation to support the business while preserving clear human accountability.
