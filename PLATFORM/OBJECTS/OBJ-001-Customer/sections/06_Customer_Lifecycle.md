# 06 — Customer Lifecycle

---

# Overview

The Customer Lifecycle defines the complete journey of a customer within Swissbay, from initial identification through to long-term relationship management.

The purpose of the lifecycle is to ensure that every customer follows a structured, measurable and repeatable journey.

Rather than treating every interaction as an isolated event, Swissbay views customer relationships as evolving partnerships that develop over time.

Every stage has clear entry criteria, exit criteria, ownership responsibilities and expected business outcomes.

This lifecycle provides the operational framework used by Sales, Customer Success, Finance, Operations and Artificial Intelligence.

---

# Lifecycle Philosophy

Swissbay does not define success as winning a sale.

Swissbay defines success as creating a profitable, long-term business relationship.

The lifecycle therefore focuses on developing trust, delivering value and continuously strengthening the commercial partnership.

A customer should naturally progress through the lifecycle as confidence and engagement increase.

# Stage 1 — Prospect

## Definition

A Prospect is an organisation that matches Swissbay's target market but has not yet entered the active sales process.

Prospects may be identified through:

* referrals
* networking
* exhibitions
* LinkedIn
* website enquiries
* supplier introductions
* market research
* industry associations

### Entry Criteria

* Fits target customer profile.
* Contact information available.

### Exit Criteria

* Converted to Lead.
* Removed as unsuitable.

### Owner

Sales

---

# Stage 2 — Lead

## Definition

A Lead is a Prospect that has entered the Swissbay sales process.

A Lead has demonstrated interest or has been selected for active engagement.

### Typical Activities

* Initial contact
* Discovery meeting
* Needs analysis
* Qualification

### Entry Criteria

* Prospect accepted by Sales.

### Exit Criteria

* Qualified
* Disqualified

### Owner

Sales

---

# Stage 3 — Qualified Lead

## Definition

A Qualified Lead has demonstrated genuine commercial potential.

Qualification considers factors such as:

* need
* budget
* authority
* timeline
* strategic fit

### Entry Criteria

* Qualification complete.

### Exit Criteria

* Opportunity created.
* Lead disqualified.

---

# Stage 4 — Opportunity

## Definition

An Opportunity represents a realistic commercial opportunity with a defined business need.

Swissbay understands:

* customer requirements
* proposed solution
* expected value
* decision timeline

### Activities

* Solution design
* Supplier engagement
* Cost estimation
* Proposal preparation

### Exit Criteria

Quotation prepared.

---

# Stage 5 — Quotation

## Definition

A formal commercial proposal has been issued.

The quotation defines:

* products
* pricing
* delivery
* commercial terms

### Outcomes

* Accepted
* Rejected
* Revised
* Expired

---

# Stage 6 — Approved Customer

## Definition

The organisation is now recognised as an official Swissbay Customer.

A customer account is created.

Commercial information becomes governed by Swissbay Nexus.

### Activities

* Customer onboarding
* Credit approval
* Payment terms
* Account assignment
* Delivery information

---

# Stage 7 — First Order

The first confirmed order represents the beginning of the commercial relationship.

Objectives include:

* flawless execution
* accurate communication
* positive first experience

The first order establishes trust.

---

# Stage 8 — Active Customer

The Customer regularly purchases products or services.

Swissbay focuses on:

* excellent service
* communication
* operational efficiency
* proactive account management

---

# Stage 9 — Repeat Customer

A Repeat Customer has demonstrated confidence in Swissbay through multiple successful transactions.

Objectives include:

* increasing share of wallet
* improving relationship depth
* identifying cross-selling opportunities
* strengthening loyalty

---

# Stage 10 — Strategic Customer

Strategic Customers contribute significant long-term value.

Selection may consider:

* annual revenue
* growth potential
* industry influence
* innovation
* partnership opportunities

Strategic Customers receive increased executive attention.

---

# Stage 11 — Preferred Customer

Preferred Customers have earned enhanced commercial status through:

* long-term partnership
* reliable payment
* collaborative relationship
* strategic importance

Benefits may include:

* priority service
* executive reviews
* proactive planning
* customised solutions

---

# Stage 12 — Dormant Customer

A Dormant Customer has not purchased within the agreed inactivity period.

Dormancy does not imply relationship failure.

Instead it triggers:

* account review
* re-engagement campaign
* executive review
* AI recommendations

---

# Stage 13 — Reactivated Customer

A Dormant Customer becomes active again through successful engagement.

Swissbay analyses:

* why the customer became dormant
* what caused reactivation
* lessons for future retention

---

# Stage 14 — Archived Customer

Archived Customers are no longer actively managed.

Reasons may include:

* business closure
* acquisition
* strategic exit
* permanent inactivity

Archived Customers remain available for:

* reporting
* auditing
* historical analysis
* AI learning
* executive review

Commercial history must never be deleted.

---

# Lifecycle KPIs

Swissbay should monitor:

* Prospect-to-Lead Conversion
* Lead Qualification Rate
* Opportunity Win Rate
* Quote Conversion Rate
* Time to First Order
* Customer Retention
* Repeat Purchase Rate
* Strategic Customer Growth
* Dormant Customer Rate
* Reactivation Rate
* Customer Lifetime Value

---

# AI Usage

Artificial Intelligence should continuously monitor lifecycle progression.

AI Agents may:

* recommend progression to the next stage
* identify stalled opportunities
* detect dormant customers
* recommend follow-up actions
* estimate customer health
* predict churn risk
* identify expansion opportunities

AI recommendations remain advisory and must comply with AI-000.

---

# Lifecycle Governance

Progression between lifecycle stages must be supported by evidence.

Examples include:

* completed qualification
* accepted quotation
* confirmed order
* approved customer account
* repeat purchases

Lifecycle stages must never be changed solely to improve reporting metrics.

The lifecycle reflects the actual commercial relationship and must remain an accurate representation of reality.

---

# Lifecycle Summary

The Customer Lifecycle transforms customer management from a collection of disconnected activities into a governed business process.

It provides Swissbay with a consistent framework for acquiring, developing, retaining and growing customer relationships while ensuring that every department and AI Agent understands exactly where each customer sits within the commercial journey.

This lifecycle forms the operational backbone of Swissbay's customer management strategy and supports sustainable, measurable business growth.
