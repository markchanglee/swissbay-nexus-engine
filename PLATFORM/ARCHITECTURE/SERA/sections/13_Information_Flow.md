# 13 — Information Flow

---

# Overview

The Information Flow Architecture defines how enterprise information moves throughout the Swissbay Enterprise Operating System.

It describes the lifecycle of information from creation through validation, governance, storage, processing, intelligence, reporting and archival.

Swissbay treats information as a governed enterprise asset whose movement follows consistent architectural principles regardless of the Business Object, application or industry.

Information flow represents the movement of enterprise knowledge—not application data.

---

# Purpose

The Information Flow Architecture exists to:

- establish a canonical information lifecycle
- strengthen data governance
- improve enterprise consistency
- enable Artificial Intelligence
- support enterprise analytics
- preserve information quality
- simplify integrations

Every information asset follows the same governed lifecycle.

---

## Validated Information

Information verified by the Validation Engine.

---

## Governed Information

Information approved through Business Rules and Workflow.

---

## Canonical Information

---

## Semantic Information

The Knowledge Graph establishes relationships.

## Intelligent Information

# Information Ownership

Every information asset requires:

- Business Owner
- Data Steward
- Governance Owner
- Security Classification
- Retention Policy

Ownership ensures accountability across the enterprise.

---

# Information Consumers

Enterprise information may be consumed by:

- Applications
- Platform Services
- AI Agents
- Reports
- Dashboards
- Industry Extensions
- External Integrations

Every consumer accesses governed information.

---

# Information Quality

Quality is continuously protected through:

- Validation Engine
- Business Rules Engine
- Audit Service
- Metadata Registry
- Data Stewardship

Quality is maintained throughout the lifecycle rather than checked only at creation.

---

# Architectural Decisions

The Information Flow Architecture is based on the following decisions.

| Decision | Rationale |
|----------|-----------|
| Business Objects are canonical | Establishes one enterprise source of truth |
| Metadata is separate from data | Preserves semantic consistency |
| Knowledge Graph enriches information | Enables enterprise reasoning |
| AI consumes governed information | Prevents unreliable recommendations |
| Reporting consumes canonical information | Ensures consistent enterprise metrics |

---

# Design Principles

Information Flow should remain:

- canonical
- governed
- explainable
- reusable
- secure
- discoverable
- technology independent

Enterprise information should outlive individual applications.

---

# Summary

The Information Flow Architecture defines how information moves through the Swissbay Enterprise Operating System.

By transforming operational data into governed knowledge and enterprise intelligence through a consistent architectural lifecycle, Swissbay establishes a trusted information foundation for intelligent enterprise operations.
