# 01 — Executive Overview

---

# Overview

The Swissbay Enterprise Reference Architecture (SERA) defines the architectural blueprint for designing, building and operating intelligent enterprise solutions on the Swissbay platform.

Rather than prescribing a specific technology stack, SERA provides a technology-independent reference architecture that standardises enterprise design across industries, deployment models and implementation teams.

It establishes the common architectural language that connects Foundation Standards, Business Objects, Platform Services and Enterprise Applications into one coherent operating model.

---

# Purpose

The Executive Overview exists to:

- introduce the Swissbay architecture
- explain the architectural philosophy
- establish a common understanding
- align business and technology stakeholders
- define the scope of SERA
- position Swissbay as an Enterprise Operating System

SERA serves as the primary architectural reference for all Swissbay implementations.

---

# Architectural Vision

Swissbay is designed as an **Enterprise Operating System**.

Unlike traditional enterprise software, Swissbay separates:

- business knowledge
- platform behaviour
- implementation technology

This separation enables organisations to evolve independently across each architectural layer while maintaining consistency and governance.

---

# Enterprise Challenge

Modern organisations often face:

- duplicated business logic
- fragmented data
- disconnected applications
- inconsistent governance
- expensive integrations
- isolated AI initiatives
- slow digital transformation

These challenges increase operational complexity and reduce organisational agility.

---

# Swissbay Solution

Swissbay addresses these challenges through four architectural layers.

```text
Foundation

↓

Business Architecture

↓

Platform Architecture

↓

Deployment Architecture
```

Each layer has a clearly defined responsibility and evolves independently within governed architectural boundaries.

---

# Architectural Layers

## Foundation

Provides:

- constitutional principles
- governance standards
- design rules
- enterprise policies

Foundation defines **how Swissbay should be built**.

---

## Business Architecture

Provides:

- canonical Business Objects
- business domains
- enterprise relationships
- business capabilities

Business Architecture defines **what the enterprise is**.

---

## Platform Architecture

Provides:

- workflow orchestration
- business rules
- validation
- automation
- artificial intelligence
- integrations
- security
- reporting

Platform Architecture defines **how the enterprise operates**.

---

## Deployment Architecture

Provides:

- cloud deployment
- infrastructure
- runtime environments
- scalability
- operational management

Deployment Architecture defines **where Swissbay runs**.

---

# Enterprise Operating Model

Swissbay follows a layered operating model.

```text
Business Strategy

↓

Foundation

↓

Business Architecture

↓

Platform Services

↓

Applications

↓

Industry Solutions
```

Each layer consumes services from the layer below while exposing capabilities to the layer above.

# Strategic Objectives

The Swissbay architecture aims to:

- establish one enterprise operating model
- minimise duplicated implementation
- support intelligent automation
- simplify enterprise integration
- strengthen governance
- enable rapid industry expansion
- remain technology independent

---

# Success Measures

The architecture is considered successful when:

- Business Objects remain canonical.
- Platform Services are consistently reused.
- Industry solutions extend rather than replace the platform.
- Artificial Intelligence operates under governance.
- New enterprise capabilities can be delivered rapidly.

---

# Executive Summary

The Swissbay Enterprise Reference Architecture provides the strategic blueprint for building intelligent, governed and reusable enterprise solutions.

By separating business knowledge, platform behaviour and implementation technology into clearly defined architectural layers, SERA establishes a scalable enterprise operating model capable of supporting multiple industries while preserving one consistent architectural vision.
