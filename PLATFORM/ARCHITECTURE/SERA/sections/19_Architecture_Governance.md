# 19 — Architecture Governance

---

# Overview

Architecture Governance defines the processes, responsibilities and decision-making framework that preserve the integrity of the Swissbay Enterprise Reference Architecture (SERA).

As Swissbay evolves, new Business Objects, Platform Services, Industry Packs and Artificial Intelligence capabilities will continually be introduced. Without governance, architectural consistency would gradually decline.

Architecture Governance ensures that innovation occurs within an agreed architectural framework while preserving long-term stability, interoperability and maintainability.

---

# Purpose

Architecture Governance exists to:

- preserve architectural integrity
- guide architectural evolution
- ensure consistency across implementations
- manage architectural change
- support compliance
- protect long-term maintainability
- enable sustainable innovation

Governance protects the architecture without preventing progress.

---

# Governance Philosophy

Swissbay follows one governing principle.

> **Innovation is encouraged. Architectural fragmentation is not.**

Every architectural decision should strengthen the platform rather than create isolated solutions.

# Governance Model

```text
Enterprise Vision

↓

Architecture Principles

↓

Architecture Standards

↓

Reference Architecture (SERA)

↓

Business Objects

↓

Platform Services

↓

Industry Packs

↓

Enterprise Solutions
```

Every layer inherits governance from the layers above it.

---

# Governance Responsibilities

Architecture Governance oversees:

- architectural standards
- Business Object lifecycle
- Platform Service evolution
- Industry Pack certification
- AI governance
- security compliance
- reference implementations
- technology strategy

Governance ensures alignment across the entire Swissbay ecosystem.

---

# Decision Authority

Architectural decisions are made collaboratively.

| Role | Responsibility |
|-------|----------------|
| Enterprise Architecture Board | Strategic architectural direction |
| Platform Architecture Team | Runtime architecture |
| Business Domain Owners | Business Object governance |
| Security Architecture Team | Security standards |
| AI Governance Committee | AI policy and model governance |
| Solution Architects | Solution-level implementation |

Each role has clearly defined responsibilities and accountability.

---

# Change Management

Architectural change follows a governed lifecycle.

```text
Proposal

↓

Architecture Review

↓

Impact Assessment

↓

Approval

↓

Implementation

↓

Validation

↓

Publication
```

No architectural change becomes canonical without review and approval.

---

# Architecture Compliance

Solutions are assessed against SERA using the following criteria:

- alignment with Business Objects
- platform service reuse
- security compliance
- governance compliance
- extensibility
- observability
- technology independence

Compliance reviews occur throughout the solution lifecycle.

---

# Architectural Decisions

Swissbay formally records major architectural decisions.

Each decision should include:

- context
- problem statement
- decision
- alternatives considered
- rationale
- expected consequences
- review date

This creates a permanent architectural knowledge base.

---

# Architecture Metrics

Governance measures:

- platform reuse
- duplicated capabilities
- extension compatibility
- architectural compliance
- AI governance adherence
- security posture
- upgrade compatibility

Metrics guide continuous architectural improvement.

---

# Governance Principles

Architecture Governance should remain:

- transparent
- collaborative
- measurable
- technology independent
- business aligned
- future focused

Governance exists to enable quality—not bureaucracy.

# Summary

Architecture Governance ensures that Swissbay evolves in a controlled and sustainable manner.

By establishing clear ownership, decision-making processes and compliance mechanisms, Swissbay preserves one coherent enterprise architecture while enabling continuous innovation across industries, technologies and implementation teams.
