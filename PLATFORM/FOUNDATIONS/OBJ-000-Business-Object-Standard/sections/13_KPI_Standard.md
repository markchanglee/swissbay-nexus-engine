# 13 — KPI Standard

---

# Purpose

The KPI (Key Performance Indicator) Standard defines how business performance is measured, governed and reported throughout Swissbay Nexus.

KPIs convert business activities into measurable outcomes.

Every KPI should help the organisation understand whether it is progressing towards its strategic and operational objectives.

KPIs are not simply numbers.

They are business measurements that drive action.

---

# Objective

The objectives of this standard are to:

- establish consistent performance measurement
- align metrics across departments
- support executive decision-making
- improve accountability
- enable AI-assisted performance analysis
- provide one version of the truth
- drive continuous improvement

---

# Philosophy

Swissbay measures outcomes rather than activity.

A KPI should answer a meaningful business question.

If a KPI does not influence a decision or behaviour, it should be challenged.

KPIs should always support the organisation's strategy.

---

# KPI Principles

## Principle 1 — Business Aligned

Every KPI must support a business objective.

KPIs should never exist simply because the data is available.

---

## Principle 2 — Measurable

Every KPI must have a clearly defined calculation.

Results should be objective and reproducible.

---

## Principle 3 — Actionable

A KPI should enable someone to take action.

Poor performance should trigger investigation or improvement.

---

## Principle 4 — Governed

Every KPI should reference governed Business Objects.

Manual calculations outside the governance framework should be avoided.

---

## Principle 5 — Explainable

Users should understand:

- what is measured
- why it matters
- how it is calculated
- who owns it

Every KPI should be transparent.

---

# Standard KPI Structure

Every KPI should contain the following information.

---

## KPI Metadata

- KPI ID
- KPI Name
- Version
- Business Owner
- Reporting Frequency
- Status

---

## Purpose

Describe what the KPI measures and why it is important.

---

## Business Objective

Identify the strategic or operational objective supported by the KPI.

---

## Business Question

Every KPI should answer a business question.

Examples:

- Are we growing?
- Are customers satisfied?
- Are suppliers performing?
- Are invoices being paid on time?

---

## Formula

Define the official calculation.

Example

```
Customer Retention Rate

=

Customers Retained

÷

Customers at Start of Period

×

100
```

The formula becomes the authoritative definition.

---

## Data Sources

Every KPI should identify:

- Business Objects
- Data Fields
- Related Business Rules

Only governed information may be used.

---

## Targets

Every KPI should define:

- Current Value
- Target Value
- Warning Threshold
- Critical Threshold

Thresholds should support proactive management.

---

## Owner

Every KPI must have a Business Owner responsible for:

- definition
- accuracy
- review
- continuous improvement

---

## Reporting Frequency

Typical frequencies include:

- Real Time
- Hourly
- Daily
- Weekly
- Monthly
- Quarterly
- Annually

Frequency should align with business needs.

Every KPI follows a lifecycle.

```
Proposed

↓

Defined

↓

Approved

↓

Measured

↓

Reviewed

↓

Improved

↓

Retired
```

KPIs should evolve with business strategy.

---

# KPI Hierarchy

KPIs should support different organisational levels.

```
Strategic KPIs

↓

Department KPIs

↓

Team KPIs

↓

Operational Metrics
```

Lower-level metrics should contribute to higher-level objectives.

---

# KPI Relationships

KPIs should reference:

- Business Objects
- Dashboards
- Business Rules
- Workflows
- AI Recommendations

Relationships improve traceability and explainability.

---

# AI Integration

Artificial Intelligence may:

- analyse KPI trends
- explain changes
- identify anomalies
- forecast future performance
- recommend corrective actions
- prioritise areas requiring attention

AI recommendations should always reference governed KPIs.

---

# KPI Validation

Before approval verify that:

- the KPI has a clear purpose
- the formula is defined
- ownership is assigned
- data sources are governed
- reporting frequency is defined
- thresholds are documented
- business value is demonstrated

---

# Common Mistakes

Avoid:

- measuring activity instead of outcomes
- undefined formulas
- duplicated KPIs
- inconsistent calculations
- KPIs without owners
- KPIs that nobody uses

Performance measurement should drive improvement.

---

# Review Questions

Governance reviewers should ask:

- Does this KPI support a business objective?
- Is the calculation unambiguous?
- Can the KPI be independently verified?
- Is ownership clearly assigned?
- Will this KPI influence business decisions?

---

# Deliverable

Every KPI within Swissbay Nexus must comply with this standard.

KPIs become governed business assets that provide consistent, reliable and measurable insight into organisational performance.

---

# Standard Summary

The KPI Standard establishes a consistent framework for defining, governing and measuring business performance across Swissbay Nexus.

By ensuring that every KPI has a clear purpose, governed calculation, accountable owner and measurable business value, Swissbay creates a trusted performance management framework that supports strategic planning, operational excellence and AI-assisted decision-making.

KPIs transform business activity into measurable outcomes, enabling continuous improvement across the organisation.
