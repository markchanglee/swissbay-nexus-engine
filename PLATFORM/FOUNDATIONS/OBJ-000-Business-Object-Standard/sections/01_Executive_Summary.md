# 01 — Executive Summary Standard

---

# Purpose

The Executive Summary is the first section of every Business Object within Swissbay Nexus.

Its purpose is to provide readers with a clear understanding of the Business Object before they read the detailed specification.

The Executive Summary answers one fundamental question:

> **"What is this object, why does it exist and why should the business care?"**

Every Business Object must begin with an Executive Summary.

---

# Objective

The objective of the Executive Summary is to provide a concise but complete business-level explanation of the Business Object.

It should enable:

- Executives to understand business value.
- Employees to understand business purpose.
- Developers to understand implementation context.
- AI Agents to understand operational meaning.

The Executive Summary establishes the business context that every subsequent section expands upon.

---

# Philosophy

Business Objects should never begin with technical information.

People understand businesses before they understand systems.

For this reason the Executive Summary always introduces the business concept before discussing workflows, data models or technology.

The Executive Summary should answer:

- What is this object?
- Why does it exist?
- Why is it important?
- How does it create value?
- Who uses it?
- How does it fit into Swissbay?

---

# Scope

The Executive Summary describes the Business Object at a high level.

It should not contain:

- Database schemas
- API definitions
- Validation rules
- Detailed workflows
- Security permissions
- Technical implementation

Those topics belong in later sections.

---

# Mandatory Content

Every Executive Summary must contain the following sections.

---

## Overview

Describe the Business Object in plain business language.

The reader should understand the object without any technical knowledge.

---

## Business Purpose

Explain why the object exists.

Describe the business problem it solves.

---

## Business Importance

Explain why the object is valuable to Swissbay.

Discuss:

- commercial value
- operational value
- strategic value
- organisational value

---

## Primary Users

Identify which departments interact with the object.

Examples:

- Sales
- Finance
- Operations
- Procurement
- Customer Success
- Executive Management
- AI Agents

---

## Relationship to the Business

Explain how the object contributes to Swissbay's operating model.

Describe where it fits within the business.

---

## AI Perspective

Briefly explain how Artificial Intelligence interacts with the object.

Do not include implementation details.

Simply describe AI's role.

---

## Executive Conclusion

Summarise why the object matters.

The conclusion should reinforce the strategic importance of the object.

---

# Writing Standards

Executive Summaries should be written using the following principles.

## Business First

Explain the business before the technology.

---

## Executive Language

Write for executives rather than developers.

Avoid technical jargon where possible.

---

## Clear Structure

Use logical headings.

Avoid long unstructured paragraphs.

---

## Neutral Tone

Describe the Business Object objectively.

Avoid marketing language or exaggerated claims.

---

## Future Ready

Write in a way that remains relevant as the business grows.

Avoid references to specific software products unless absolutely necessary.

---

# Quality Standard

A completed Executive Summary should allow a reader to understand:

- the purpose of the object
- why the object exists
- who uses it
- why it matters
- how it supports Swissbay

without reading any other section.

---

# Common Mistakes

Avoid:

- discussing implementation details
- explaining database tables
- describing APIs
- including workflow diagrams
- duplicating information from later sections
- writing from a technical perspective only

The Executive Summary introduces the object.

It should not attempt to document the entire object.

---

# AI Generation Guidance

When generating an Executive Summary, AI should:

- explain the object in business language
- introduce the business value
- avoid implementation details
- maintain a professional tone
- ensure the summary can stand alone

AI should never invent business processes that are not defined elsewhere in the specification.

---

# Validation Checklist

Before approving an Executive Summary, verify that:

- The object is clearly defined.
- The business purpose is explained.
- Business value is described.
- Departments are identified.
- AI usage is mentioned.
- No technical implementation details are included.
- The document can be understood independently.

All checklist items should pass before the section is considered complete.

---

# Review Questions

During governance review, ask:

- Does this summary explain the object clearly?
- Would a new employee understand the object?
- Would an executive understand its value?
- Does the summary avoid unnecessary technical detail?
- Does it align with the Swissbay Nexus Constitution?

---

# Deliverable

Every Business Object must include one Executive Summary that conforms to this standard.

This document serves as the reference specification for writing Executive Summaries across the Swissbay Nexus platform.

No Business Object should omit this section.

---

# Standard Summary

The Executive Summary establishes the business identity of every Business Object.

It introduces the object, explains its purpose and demonstrates its value to Swissbay before any technical or operational details are discussed.

By following this standard, every Business Object begins with a consistent, executive-level explanation that supports employees, leadership, developers and Artificial Intelligence alike.

The Executive Summary is therefore the foundation upon which every Business Object specification is built.
