# 11 — AI Standard

---

# Purpose

The AI Standard defines how Artificial Intelligence is designed, governed and integrated throughout the Swissbay Nexus platform.

Artificial Intelligence is not considered an external tool.

Within Swissbay Nexus, AI is treated as a governed business capability that supports employees, departments and Business Objects while remaining accountable to organisational governance.

This standard ensures that AI behaves consistently, transparently and responsibly across every Business Object.

---

# Objective

The objectives of this standard are to:

- standardise AI behaviour
- support responsible AI
- improve employee productivity
- maintain business governance
- enable explainable decision support
- protect business information
- ensure consistent AI implementation

---

# Philosophy

Swissbay believes Artificial Intelligence should augment human capability rather than replace human accountability.

AI should:

- assist
- analyse
- recommend
- summarise
- predict
- automate repetitive work

AI should never assume ownership of business decisions.

Final accountability always remains with authorised people.

---

# AI Principles

## Principle 1 — Human First

Artificial Intelligence assists people.

It does not replace business ownership or management responsibility.

---

## Principle 2 — Explainability

Every AI recommendation should be explainable.

AI should reference the business information used to produce its recommendation.

Employees should understand:

- why
- how
- what information

was used.

---

## Principle 3 — Governed Information

AI should only use approved Business Objects.

AI should never invent governed business facts.

---

## Principle 4 — Permission Aware

AI inherits the permissions of the user requesting assistance.

AI should never expose information that the user is not authorised to access.

---

## Principle 5 — Transparency

Users should always know when content has been generated or enhanced by AI.

AI-generated outputs should be distinguishable from governed business records.

---

## Principle 6 — Continuous Learning

Swissbay continuously improves AI capabilities through governance, feedback and operational experience.

Business Objects remain the source of truth.

AI improves its understanding—not the governed facts themselves.

---

# AI Roles

Swissbay AI performs four primary roles.

---

## Assistant

Supports employees by:

- answering questions
- summarising information
- drafting communications
- locating Business Objects
- explaining procedures

---

## Analyst

Analyses business information.

Examples include:

- customer trends
- supplier performance
- financial anomalies
- operational bottlenecks
- project health

---

## Advisor

Provides recommendations.

Examples:

- next best action
- customer follow-up
- supplier selection
- risk mitigation
- resource allocation

Recommendations require human review.

---

## Automation Partner

Works with workflow engines to:

- prepare documents
- classify requests
- enrich records
- trigger notifications
- recommend automations

AI supports automation but does not replace governance.

---

# AI Capability Levels

Swissbay defines five capability levels.

| Level | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| AI-1 | Information Retrieval |
| AI-2 | Analysis |
| AI-3 | Recommendations |
| AI-4 | Assisted Automation |
| AI-5 | Autonomous Assistance (Governed) |

Higher capability levels require stronger governance.

---

# AI Inputs

AI may consume:

- Business Objects
- Workflows
- Business Rules
- KPIs
- Dashboards
- Documents
- Meeting Notes
- Knowledge Graph relationships

AI should clearly distinguish between:

- governed facts
- inferred insights
- predictions

---

# AI Outputs

AI may produce:

- summaries
- recommendations
- classifications
- forecasts
- draft communications
- action plans
- meeting briefs
- executive reports

Outputs should always indicate that they were AI-generated.

---

# AI Decision Boundaries

AI may:

- recommend
- predict
- classify
- explain
- prioritise
- summarise

AI may not:

- approve contracts
- authorise payments
- dismiss employees
- alter governed data
- override Business Rules
- bypass security

Critical business decisions require authorised human approval.

---

# AI Explainability Standard

Every significant AI recommendation should include:

- Recommendation
- Supporting Business Objects
- Business Rules Referenced
- Confidence Score
- Assumptions
- Alternative Options

Explainability builds trust.

---

# AI Governance

Every AI capability must define:

- Business Owner
- Technical Owner
- Data Sources
- Permissions
- Review Frequency
- Monitoring Requirements
- Escalation Process

AI remains subject to governance.

---

# AI Monitoring

Swissbay should monitor:

- Recommendation Accuracy
- User Acceptance Rate
- False Positives
- False Negatives
- Response Time
- Usage Frequency
- Escalations
- Hallucination Incidents

These metrics support continuous improvement.

---

# AI Security

AI must:

- respect user permissions
- protect confidential information
- log significant actions
- prevent data leakage
- comply with security standards

AI should never expose information outside authorised boundaries.

---

# AI Ethics

Swissbay AI should operate according to the following ethical principles:

- Fairness
- Transparency
- Accountability
- Privacy
- Reliability
- Human Oversight

AI should avoid bias wherever practical and operate consistently across all users.

---

# AI Integration

AI should integrate with:

- Business Objects
- Workflows
- Dashboards
- Automation
- Knowledge Graph
- Search
- APIs
- Analytics

AI is a platform capability rather than a standalone application.

---

# AI Validation Rules

Before approval verify that:

- AI purpose is defined
- business owner is assigned
- permissions are documented
- decision boundaries are clear
- explainability exists
- monitoring is defined
- governance has been completed

---

# Common Mistakes

Avoid:

- allowing AI to become the source of truth
- giving AI unrestricted permissions
- using AI without explainability
- automating decisions that require human judgement
- exposing confidential information

AI should always support the business—not replace it.

---

# Review Questions

Governance reviewers should ask:

- Is AI solving a genuine business problem?
- Can the recommendation be explained?
- Does AI respect permissions?
- Is human accountability preserved?
- Does AI align with the Swissbay Constitution?

---

# Deliverable

Every Business Object that incorporates Artificial Intelligence must comply with this standard.

AI should operate as a governed business capability that enhances productivity while maintaining transparency, security and accountability.

---

# Standard Summary

The AI Standard defines how Artificial Intelligence is responsibly integrated into Swissbay Nexus.

By establishing clear principles, governance, permissions and decision boundaries, Swissbay ensures that AI remains a trusted business capability that augments human expertise without compromising governance or accountability.

Artificial Intelligence becomes a strategic partner within the business operating platform rather than an uncontrolled technology feature.
