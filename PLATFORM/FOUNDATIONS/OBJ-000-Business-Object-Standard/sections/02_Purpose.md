# 02 — Purpose Standard

---

# Purpose

The Purpose section defines why a Business Object exists within the Swissbay Nexus platform.

Every Business Object must have a clearly defined purpose before any workflows, data structures or automation are designed.

The Purpose section answers the question:

> **"Why does this Business Object exist?"**

A Business Object without a clearly defined purpose introduces ambiguity into the operating model.

---

# Objective

The objective of this section is to establish a single business purpose that guides every future decision relating to the Business Object.

The Purpose becomes the reference point for:

- Business Rules
- Workflows
- Data Models
- AI Behaviour
- Dashboards
- KPIs
- Automation
- Future Enhancements

Every subsequent section should support the Purpose defined here.

---

# Philosophy

Swissbay believes that Business Objects exist to solve business problems rather than represent software entities.

Technology changes.

Business purpose should remain stable.

For this reason, the Purpose section should describe the business outcome the object exists to achieve rather than how software implements it.

---

# Scope

The Purpose section explains:

- why the object exists
- what business problem it solves
- what value it creates
- how it supports Swissbay

It should not include:

- technical implementation
- database design
- workflows
- APIs
- integrations
- security configuration

These topics belong in later sections.

---

# Mandatory Content

Every Purpose section should include the following components.

---

## Purpose Statement

Provide a clear and concise statement explaining why the Business Object exists.

This statement should be understandable by executives, employees and AI Agents.

---

## Business Problem

Describe the problem that would exist if this Business Object did not exist.

Examples include:

- duplicated information
- inconsistent reporting
- fragmented processes
- poor governance
- operational inefficiency

---

## Business Objective

Describe the measurable outcome that the Business Object enables.

Examples include:

- improve customer management
- govern supplier relationships
- manage inventory
- coordinate projects
- manage contracts

---

## Strategic Purpose

Explain how the Business Object contributes to Swissbay's long-term strategy.

Discuss:

- growth
- scalability
- governance
- decision-making
- AI readiness

---

## Operational Purpose

Describe how day-to-day operations benefit from the Business Object.

Focus on business activities rather than software functionality.

---

## Relationship to the Operating Model

Explain how the object supports the Swissbay operating model.

Describe where it fits within the organisation.

---

## AI Perspective

Briefly explain how AI benefits from the existence of this Business Object.

Do not discuss implementation.

Focus on business capability.

---

## Success Criteria

Define how Swissbay will know that the Business Object is fulfilling its intended purpose.

Examples include:

- improved consistency
- reduced duplication
- faster decision-making
- better reporting
- improved customer outcomes

---

# Writing Standards

Purpose sections should follow these principles.

---

## Business First

Describe the business before discussing technology.

---

## Outcome Focused

Explain the outcome created by the object rather than the tasks it performs.

---

## Timeless

Avoid references to current software products or technologies.

The Purpose should remain relevant for many years.

---

## Clear Language

Use plain business language.

Avoid unnecessary technical terminology.

---

## Strategic Thinking

The Purpose should explain why the object matters to the organisation rather than merely describing its contents.

---

# Quality Standard

A completed Purpose section should allow a reader to understand:

- why the object exists
- what business problem it solves
- what value it creates
- how it supports Swissbay
- why it deserves to exist as a Business Object

without reading the remainder of the specification.

---

# Common Mistakes

Avoid:

- describing fields
- describing workflows
- describing APIs
- describing user interfaces
- repeating the Executive Summary
- explaining implementation details

The Purpose explains "why."

Other sections explain "how."

---

# AI Generation Guidance

When generating a Purpose section, AI should:

- focus on business outcomes
- avoid implementation details
- explain long-term business value
- maintain executive-level language
- align with the Swissbay Nexus Constitution

AI should not invent business objectives that are not supported elsewhere in the specification.

---

# Validation Checklist

Before approving the Purpose section, verify that:

- The business problem is clearly defined.
- The purpose is easy to understand.
- Strategic value is explained.
- Operational value is explained.
- AI relevance is identified.
- No implementation details are included.
- The Purpose aligns with the Executive Summary.

---

# Review Questions

Governance reviewers should ask:

- Does the object need to exist?
- Is the business problem clearly described?
- Does the Purpose align with Swissbay strategy?
- Would a new employee understand why this object exists?
- Could every later section trace back to this Purpose?

---

# Deliverable

Every Business Object must contain one Purpose section that follows this standard.

The Purpose should become the guiding principle for the entire Business Object and remain stable throughout its lifecycle.

---

# Standard Summary

The Purpose section defines the reason a Business Object exists.

It explains the business problem, desired outcomes and strategic contribution of the object while establishing the foundation upon which every other section of the specification is built.

A well-written Purpose ensures that the Business Object remains focused, valuable and aligned with the long-term vision of Swissbay Nexus.
