# Purpose

The Governance Standard defines how Business Objects, standards, workflows, dashboards, Artificial Intelligence, automations and business knowledge are governed throughout Swissbay Nexus.

Governance ensures that Swissbay evolves in a controlled, transparent and consistent manner.

It establishes who may create, approve, modify and retire business assets while preserving the integrity of the platform.

Governance protects Swissbay's knowledge.

---

# Objective

The objectives of governance are to:

- maintain consistency
- protect business knowledge
- ensure accountability
- support controlled evolution
- preserve trust
- enable enterprise scalability
- ensure every change is auditable

Governance exists to protect long-term business value.

---

# Philosophy

Swissbay treats business knowledge as an enterprise asset.

Every Business Object, Standard, Workflow and KPI represents institutional knowledge.

Institutional knowledge should evolve through governance rather than individual preference.

Governance protects continuity.

People may change.

Business knowledge remains.

Every proposed change should identify:

- affected Business Objects
- affected Workflows
- affected KPIs
- affected Dashboards
- affected AI Agents
- affected APIs
- affected Automations

The impact assessment should be documented before approval.

# Exception Management

Occasionally deviations from standards may be necessary.

Exceptions should:

- be documented
- include business justification
- include technical justification
- define review date
- define expiry date

Temporary exceptions should not become permanent architecture.

---

# Audit Requirements

Every governance action should record:

- Asset ID
- Version
- Change Summary
- Author
- Reviewer
- Approver
- Approval Date
- Release Date
- Related Issues
- Related Pull Requests

Audit history should never be deleted.

---

# AI Governance

Artificial Intelligence should support governance by:

- identifying inconsistencies
- suggesting improvements
- performing impact analysis
- checking compliance
- generating documentation
- recommending standard updates

AI recommendations remain advisory.

Governance authority remains with people.

---

# Governance Metrics

Swissbay should monitor:

- Standards Compliance
- Review Cycle Time
- Approval Time
- Number of Exceptions
- Documentation Coverage
- Audit Completion Rate
- AI Recommendation Adoption
- Platform Consistency Score

These metrics demonstrate governance maturity.

---

# Validation Rules

Before approval verify that:

- ownership is assigned
- lifecycle stage is correct
- version exists
- impact assessment completed
- approvals recorded
- documentation updated
- audit history created

---

# Common Mistakes

Avoid:

- undocumented changes
- missing approvals
- bypassing reviews
- changing standards informally
- inconsistent versioning
- orphaned Business Objects

Governance should be lightweight but never optional.

---

# Review Questions

Governance reviewers should ask:

- Does this change improve Swissbay?
- Is the impact understood?
- Are all dependencies identified?
- Is documentation complete?
- Does the change align with the Nexus Constitution?

---

# Deliverable

Every governed asset within Swissbay Nexus must comply with this Governance Standard.

Governance provides the discipline required to evolve the platform while preserving consistency, accountability and long-term business value.

# Standard Summary

The Governance Standard establishes the framework through which Swissbay Nexus evolves in a controlled and transparent manner.

By combining clear ownership, structured approvals, version control, auditability and continuous improvement, Swissbay ensures that every Business Object, Standard, Workflow, KPI, Dashboard, AI capability and Automation remains governed throughout its lifecycle.

Governance is therefore the mechanism that transforms Swissbay Nexus from a collection of documents into a sustainable business operating platform.
